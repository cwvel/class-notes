## 22.1 Electrophilic aromatic substitution
Substitution of an aryl H with an electrophile
- Halogens (-X)
- Nitro group (-NO2)
- Sulfonic acid (-SO3H)
- Alkyl or acyl group
![400](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506101700.png)
### A. Halogenation to chloro/bromoarenes
Chlorine in the presence of a Lewis acid catalyst
1. **Lewis acid-base reaction** between Cl and Lewis acid produces *chloronium ion* (strong electrophile) as part of the ion pair
2. **Nucleophilic attack** from $\pi$ system onto the chloronium ion produces resonance stabilized cation intermediate (*note (+) primarily at ortho and para positions*)
3. **Proton transfer** to regenerate Lewis acid catalyst, producing chlorobenzene and HCl
![Pasted image 20260506101740](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506101740.png)
Bromination mechanism is identical with Br2, producing bromobenzene and HBr.

General two-step mechanism:
![400](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506103927.png)

### B. Nitration and Sulfonation
#### Nitration to nitroarenes
Electrophile is the nitronium ion NO2+, formed by nitric acid + sulfiric acid.
1. **Formation of nitronium ion**
	1. Protonation of nitric acid by sulfiric acid
	2. Loss of water gives nitronium ion
2. **Electrophilic addition** (same)
	1. Nucleophilic attack from ring
	2. Proton transfer
![Pasted image 20260506101749](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506101749.png)
The nitro group can then be reduced to an amino group - useful for synthesis.
#### Sulfonation to arylsulfonic acids
The electrophile is SO3 or HSO3+ (depending on conditions)
![Pasted image 20260506101759](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506101759.png)
### C. Friedel-Crafts alkylation and acylation
#### FC alkylation
1. **Lewis acid-base reaction** between the haloalkane (lewis base) and aluminimum chloride (lewis acid), placing a positive charge on the halogen (also can be written as a carbocation for simplicity)
2. **Nucleophilic attack** from ring to carbocation gives resonance-stabilized cation intermediate
3. **Proton transfer** regenerates the aromatic ring
![Pasted image 20260506101809](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506101809.png)
Rearrangements and overalkylation can be an issue, so alkylation with primary halides is not synthetically useful.
- Can be used for introducing isopropyl, tert-butyl, and other alkyl groups that don't rearrange
- Fails on benzene rings with one or more strongly EWD groups
![300](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506104618.png)
![500](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506104756.png)
#### FC acylation
1. **Lewis acid-base reaction** the halogen on the acyl halide reacts with AlCl3, and redistribution of electrons forms an ion pair with an *acylium ion*
2. **Electrophilic addition** same as before, through resonance-stabilized cation intermediate
![Pasted image 20260506104839](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260506104839.png)
The acylium ions do not undergo rearrangements
The acylbenzene can then be reduced to the alkylbenzene through Clemmensen/Wolff-Kishner reductions

## 22.2 Disubstitution and Polysubstitution
Substituents on the aromatic ring can influence reaction rate and substitution patterns in electrophilic aromatic substitution (SEAr)
- Meta directed vs. ortho-para directed
- Speed up (activate) vs. slow down (deactivate)
![400](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513210538.png)
### Directing effects
#### Nitration/bromination of anisole
Addition to the **para** position allows a fourth contributing resonance structure that allows all atoms to have complete octets, thus it is favoured.
- Para- and ortho-directed, but majority para because ortho has more steric hinderance
![550](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513210715.png)
![Pasted image 20260513210740](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513210740.png)
We can also consider it as an "enol" type mechanism,
![200](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513210759.png)
#### Nitration of benzoic acid
Para/ortho has a resonance structure that places the positive charge on adjacent carbons, so its contribution is negligible. Thus meta is favored as it has 3 contributing resonance structures.
![Pasted image 20260513212101](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513212101.png)
![200](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513212109.png)
#### Nitration of nitrobenzene
The two nitro groups inductively *destabilize* the carbocation (deactivating)
![Pasted image 20260513212150](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513212150.png)
![Pasted image 20260513212259](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513212259.png)
Similarly the para position has a very unstable resonance structure and so meta is favored.

Almost all ortho-para directing groups have an unshared pair of electrons on the atom bonded to the ring, which allows the positive charge to be further delocalized (and thus stabilized) when attack occurs at ortho/para positions.
Alkyl groups are also ortho-para directing due to inductive polarization (hyperconjugation).
![300](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513212810.png)
### Activating/deactivating effects
1. Resonance effect that delocalizes the (+) has an *activating* effect
2. Any resonance or inductive effect that decreases electron density (EWG) has a *deactivating* effect
3. Any ERG has an *activating* effect (releases electron density toward cation intermediate)
4. Inductive effect from halogens (EWG) *deactivates* the ring

Alkyl groups/atom on ring has unshared e-pair
- ortho-para directing, electron releasing $\Rightarrow$ activating
Halogens
- ortho-para directing but electron withdrawing $\Rightarrow$ weakly deactivating
All groups with a partial positive charge on the atom
- meta-directing, electron-withdrawing $\Rightarrow$ deactivating

Electron-releasing groups are activating $\Rightarrow$ ortho-para directing
EWG other than halogens are deactivating $\Rightarrow$ meta directing
Halogens show deactivation but are ortho-para because of the stabilizing resonance effect of the unshared e-pair

#### Specific groups
1. Amine and alcohol derivatives
	- Activating (through *resonance*)
	- ortho/para-directing
2. Phenyl
	- Weakly activating (through *resonance*)
	- ortho/para-directing
3. Alkyl
	- Weakly activating (through *induction*)
	- ortho/para-directing
4. NO2 and other EWD groups
	- Deactivating (through *induction* -- destabilizing carbocation)
	- meta-directing
5. Halogens
	- *Inductively* deactivating (EWG)
	- Ortho/para-directing (due to *resonance*)
		- Positive charge on Cl allows all atoms to satisfy octet rule (additional contributing resonance structure)
**Halogens** have two contradicting directions:
- inductive effect (halogens are electronegative) of EWG are deactivating (generally stronger force)
- but the ortho-para is stabilized from the extra electron pair on the halogen
![300](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513213621.png)

**With 2+ substituents, the more activating group dominates the orientation preference.**
- This is because activating groups will activate the ring for more substitution according to their orientation preference, while deactivating groups only serve to deactivate the ring toward further substitution

#### Groups summary
**Activating**
- Strongly: -NH2, -NRH, -NRR', -OH, -OR
- Moderately: (have resonance but EWG carbonyl group)
	- -NH-(C=O)-R
	- -O-(C=O)-R
- Weakly: -R (induction), -Ar (resonance)
**Deactivating**
- Weakly: -X
*--(All above are ortho/para-directing)--*
*--(All below are meta-directing)--* 
Since one of the carbocation resonance structures are lost (see nitro example)
- Moderately: (carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acid derivatives)
	- -CHO, -(C=O)-R, -(C=O)-X, -SO3H, -CN
![200](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513213347.png)
- Strongly: -NO2, -N(+)R3 (ammonia), -CF3, -CCl3

## 22.3 Nucleophilic aromatic substitution
### A. SnAr via benzynes
![400](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513215404.png)
![200](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513215648.png)
**Mechanism.**
1. **Deprotonate and break a bond** simultaneously to lose the halogen (dehalogenation) and give a benzyne intermediate
2. **Nucleophilic addition** of amide ion to a carbon in the triple bond gives carbanion intermediate
3. **Add a proton** from ammonia

### B. SnAr by addition-elimination
Nucleophilic attack of ring with a halogen gives negatively charged Meisenheimer complex, followed by loss of halogen to give the substituted product
- requires presence of EWG ortho/para to the halogen to activate the ring toward nucleophilic attack
![400](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513220940.png)
![Pasted image 20260513220949](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260513220949.png)
**Mechanism.**
1. **Nucleophile adds to ring** at halogen carbon, placing a negative charge on the ring that is resonance-stabilized by EWG in ortho/para positions. This intermediate is called the *Meisenheimer complex*
2. **Eliminate the halide ion** to regenerate the aromatic ring and give the product

Choosing between the two, consider whether the ring is electrophilic or not.
