# 24.3 Heck reaction
Vinylic hydrogen (H on alkene) is substituted
- with haloalkene/haloarene, in base and Pd catalyst
- less substituted carbon preferred
- usually E-stereoselective
- haloalkene configuration conserved
- syn elimination in last step
	- if not possible, double bond shifts so syn elimination can occur
![Pasted image 20260604222542](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260604222542.png)
- X = I > Br > Cl, OTf
![Pasted image 20260604222821](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260604222821.png)

1) **oxidative addition** of RX gives PD(II) coordinating both R and X
2) **syn addition** to alkene (Pd less (more?) sterically demanding)
	1) intermediate rotates around central C-C bond to align H and Pd(II)X *syn* to each other
3) **syn elimination** of H and Pd(II)X gives product
4) **reductive elimination** releases acid HX (+ B) and regenerates PD catalyst

## 24.4 Catalytic allylic alkylation
- Nucleophile replaces an allylic leaving group (commonly carboxylate like acetate) in the presence of Pd
	- stereochemistry is retained due to a double inversion
- regioselectivity: prefers less substituted end of $\eta^3$-allyl complex
### Overview
![400](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260604232335.png)

### Mechanism
![Pasted image 20260604232408](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260604232408.png)
1) **coordination** of allylic species to make a $\pi$ complex
2) **oxidative addition** expels leaving group X
	1) forms $\eta^3\pi$-allyl complex
3) **nucleophilic attack** from solution onto *less substituted side* followed by **Pd dissociation**
