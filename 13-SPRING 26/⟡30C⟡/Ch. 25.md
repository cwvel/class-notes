Carbohydrates
* "hydrate" of water: $C_{12}(H_2O)_m$
	* ex. glucose $C_6(H2O)_6$, sucrose $C_{12}(H_2O)_{11}$
* not completely accurate, but the name persists
	* ex. 2-deoxy-N-acetylglucosamine $C_8H_{15}NO_6$
* the most abundant organic molecule
	* stores energy (starch, glucose, glycogen), support structures (cellulose), exoskeleton of shellfish, connective tissues (acid saccharides), component of nucleic acids
* monosaccharides are *polyhydroxyaldehyde (OH) / ketone (C=O)*

Monosaccharides
- General formula
	- $C_n(H_2O)_n$ with most common $n=3\sim 8$
	- [# C] 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8
	- [-ose] triose, tetrose, pentose, hexose, heptose, octose
	- aldehyde $\to$ aldose, ketone $\to$ ketose
- Triose
	- [aldotriose]
* Fisher projection
	* [R and S glyceraldehyde]

D- & L-Monosaccharides
- D/L-glyceraldehydes serve as reference points
- If the penultimate C (furthest away from carbonyl C) has the same configuration as the D-glyceraldehyde, then that monosaccharide is a D-sugar
