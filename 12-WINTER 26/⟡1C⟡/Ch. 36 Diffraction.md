**Fresnel and Fraunhofer diffraction**
![300](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260227114127.png)
- **Fresnel** diffraction: diffracted waves are observed close (relative to the size of the object causing the diffraction) to the place where they are diffracted
- **Fraunhofer** diffraction: source and observer are far from obstructing surface, outgoing rays can be considered parallel

# single slit diffraction
**Fraunhofer diffraction** means we can assume the light paths are essentially parallel
path-length difference is therefore
$$d=b\sin\theta$$
![200](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260304150840.png)
where $b$ = slit width
dark bands appear if $d$ is a whole number multiple of wavelengths, e.g.
$$b\sin\theta=m\lambda,\quad m=\pm1,\pm2,\pm3,\dots$$
note that $m=0$ corresponds to $\theta=0$ which is the center of the diffraction pattern, which is a bright band.
then the interference *minima* angles are:
$$\sin\theta=\frac{m\lambda}{b}$$
and the interference *maxima* are evenly spaced between each minima.
the position of the $m$-th dark band is
$$y=\frac{mx\lambda}{b}$$
- $x$ = distance to screen, $b$ = slit width

****
- **single-slit diffraction**
	- monochromatic light through a narrow slit of width $a$ produces diffraction patterns on a distant screen
		- destructive interference (dark fringe) at point $P$ at angle $\theta$: $\sin\theta=\frac{m\lambda}{a}$
		- intensity:
$$I=I_0\left\{\frac{\sin[\pi a(\sin\theta)/\lambda]}{\pi a(\sin\theta)/\lambda}\right\}^2$$

# diffraction gratings
![150](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260304155137.png)
consists of a large number of thin parallel slits, spaced $d$ apart
intensity maxima occur at:
$$d\sin\theta=m\lambda\quad(m=0,\pm1,\pm2,\dots)$$
where $d$ = distance between slits (same as two-source pattern)

maxima for grating are sharp and narrow
![300](../pasted_images/Pasted%20image%2020260227114516.png)
increasing the number of slits makes the intensity at each maxima greater but the maxima are narrower due to conservation of energy.
- peak intensity of the maxima will be proportional to $N^2$, where $N$ is the total number of slits
- the width of each maxima is proportional to $1/N$
	- total energy at the screen is roughly equal to # of maxima times the peak intensity of a maximum times the width of the maximum

****
- **x-ray diffraction**
	- crystal serves as 3D diffraction grating for x-rays with wavelengths of the same order of magnitude as spacing between atoms in the crystal
	- **bragg condition:** for set of crystal planes distance $d$ apart, constructive interference occurs when angles of incidence and scattering are *equal* and when $2d\sin\theta=m\lambda$

# circular apertures and resolving power
- diffraction pattern from circular aperture of diameter $D$ consists of a central bright spot (Airy disk) and series of concentric dark and bright rings
- angular radius $\theta_1$ of the first dark ring: $\sin\theta_1=1.22\frac{\lambda}{D}$
- equal to angular size of Airy disk
- **Rayleigh's criterion:** two point objects are just barely resolved when their angular separation $\theta$ is given by that equationx
