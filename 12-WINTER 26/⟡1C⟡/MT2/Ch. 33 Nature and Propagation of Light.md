### 33.5 polarization of light
**polarization** is a characteristic of all transverse waves.
we can create **polarized waves** from **unpolarized waves** using a polarizing filter.

**ideal polarizing filter (polarizer)** passes 100% of the incident light's $¥vec E$-components that are *parallel* to the filter's polarizing axis, while completely blocking all light that is polarized *perpendicular* to the axis

![500](Pasted%20image%2020260213142920.png)
- unpolarized light through an ideal polarizer: transmitted intensity is exactly **half** of the incident unpolarized light
	- unpolarized light is a random mixture of all possible polarization states, so *on average* it has equal components in any two perpendicular directions

passing through a second polarizer (analyzer):
![500](Pasted%20image%2020260213143116.png)

**Malus's Law**
$$I=I_¥max¥cos^2¥phi$$
- it only applies if the incident light passing through the analyzer has already been linearly polarized
- when the polarization of the second polarizer is perpendicular to the first, then $¥phi=90^¥circ$ and $I=0$ (no light transmitted)

### polarization by reflection
- **brewster's law:** when unpolarized light strikes interface between two materials, it is completely polarized perpendicular to the plane of incidence if angle of incidence equals the polarizing angle
