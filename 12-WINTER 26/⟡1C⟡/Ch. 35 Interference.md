# two-source interference of light
when two sources are *in phase*,
- **constructive interference** occurs when difference in path length is 
$$0 \text{ or } N\lambda$$
- relative to the other, one is shifted by a full # of wavelengths
- **destructive interference** occurs when difference is
$$(N+\frac{1}{2})\lambda$$
- relative to the other, one is shifted by half a wavelength

![300](Pasted%20image%2020260303171515.png)

if two sources are separated by distance $d$ and are very far from point $P$, with line to $P$ making an angle $\theta$, they travel almost parallel directions at an angle $\theta$,
- **path length** (extra distance travelled by one wave) is
$$d\sin\theta$$
- **constructive interference** occurs when the path difference is an integer # of wavelengths
$$d\sin\theta=m\lambda$$
- **destructive interference** occurs when the path difference is a half-integer # of wavelengths
$$d\sin\theta=(m+\frac{1}{2})\lambda$$
- for **small angles** $\theta$, the position $y_m$ of the $m$-th bright fringe on a screen at distance $R$ from the sources is
$$y_m=R\frac{m\lambda}{d}$$
- and the position $y_\min$ of the $m$-th minimum from the center (which is technically the $m=0$-th minimum) is
$$y_\min=R\frac{(m-\frac{1}{2})\lambda}{d}$$
![200](Pasted%20image%2020260303183059.png)
- the distance between adjacent bright bands in the pattern is *inversely proportional* to $d$ (distance between slits)

# intensity in interference patterns
when two sinusoidal waves with equal amplitude $E$ and phase difference $\phi$ are superimposed,
- each source by itself has an intensity of $\dfrac{1}{2}\epsilon_0cE^2$ at the point $P$
- if they are in phase, then the waves arriving at $P$ differ in phase by $\phi$ (proportional to difference in path lengths):
$$E_1(t)=E\cos(\omega t+\phi)$$
$$E_2(t)=E\cos\omega t$$
- resultant e-field amplitude for the two-source interference is:
	- $E$ = amplitude from one source
	- $\phi$ = phase difference between waves
$$E_P=2E\left|\cos\frac{\phi}{2}\right|$$
- the intensity in two-source interference is:
$$I=I_0\cos^2\frac{\phi}{2}$$
- the ratio of the phase difference to $2\pi$ is the ratio of the path difference to $\lambda$:
$$\frac{\phi}{2\pi}=\frac{r_2-r_1}{\lambda}$$
- rewriting, with $k=2\pi/\lambda$ (wave number)
$$\phi=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}(r_2-r_1)=k(r_2-r_1)$$


# interference in thin films
when light is reflected from both sides of a thin film of thickness $t$ and *no phase shift* occurs at either surface,
- **constructive interference** occurs when 
$$2t=m\lambda$$
- **destructive interference** occurs when 
$$2t=(m+\frac{1}{2})\lambda$$
if one of the two waves has a **half-cycle** reflection phase shift (when $n_2>n_1$), the conditions are *reversed*
- this only occurs when a ray is reflected at the interface with $n_2>n_1$
