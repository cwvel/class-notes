>[!definition]
>Let $X$ and $Y$ be discrete random variables. The **discrete bivariate distribution**
>1. $S_{X\times Y}$ is the 2-dimensional outcome space of $X\times Y$.
>2. The joint pmf, $f(x,y)=P(X=x, Y=y)$ satisfying the following properties:
>	1. $f(x,y)\geq 0$
>	2. $\sum_{(x,y)\in S_{X\times Y}}f(x,y)=1$
>	3. $P((X,Y)\in A)=\sum_{(x,y)\in A}f(x,y)$ with $A\subseteq S_{X\times Y}$
>$\quad$

**Ex.** Roll of two die $R_1$ and $R_2$.
Define $X=\text{min}(R_1,R_2)$ and $Y=\text{max}(R_1,R_2)$

$P(X=x,Y=y), 1\geq x\geq y\geq 6$

**Case:** $x=y$
$P(X=x,Y=x)=P(R_1=x,R_2=x)=\dfrac{1}{36}$ for any $x$ in $S$

**Case:** $x<y$
$R_1=x,R_2=y$ or $R_1=y,R_2=x$
$P(X=x,Y=y)=\dfrac{2}{36}=\dfrac{1}{18}$

**Case:** $x>y$
$P(X=x,Y=y)=0$

$$P(X=x,Y=y)=\begin{cases}\dfrac{1}{36},\quad x=y\\\dfrac{1}{18},\quad x<y\\0,\quad x>y\end{cases}$$

>[!definition]
>Given a joint pmf of $X,Y$ on $X_{X\times Y}$, the pmf of $X$ itself is called the **marginal pmf** of $X$ (fix $Y$ and vary $X$) and is given by:
>$$f_X(x)=P(X=x)=\sum_{y\in S_Y}f(x,y)$$

>[!definition] 
>$X$, $Y$ are two independent random variabels if and only if
>$$P(X=x,Y=y)=P(X=x)P(Y=y)$$ for $(x,y)\in S_{X\times Y}$
>$\implies$ $$f(x,y)=f_X(x)f_Y(y)$$

### Expectation
1. The expected value $E(u(X,Y))$ is given by
$$E(u(X,Y))=\sum_{S_{X\times Y}}u(x,y)f(x,y)$$
2. Marginal mean: $$\mu_X=E(X), \mu(X,Y)=X$$
3. Marginal variance: $$\sigma_X^2=E((x-\mu_X)^2)$$
