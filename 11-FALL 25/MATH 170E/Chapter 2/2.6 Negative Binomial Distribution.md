>[!definition]
>Consider the following experiment of performing *Bernoulli trials* with success probability $p$ until $r$ successes occur ($r$ is a fixed positive integer). $X$ is the number of trials needed to observe $r$ successes. Then $X$ is called the **negative binomial distribution** and is denoted
>$$X\sim\text{NB}(r,p)$$

Remark: $r=1$, then $X=$ # of trials to observe first success $\sim\text{Geom}(p)$

$X\sim\text{NB}(r,p), k\geq r$
$$\boxed{P(X=k)=f(k)=\dbinom{k-1}{r-1}p^r(1-p)^{k-r}}$$
**Explanation**
Given $k\geq r$, $P(X=k)=?$
$k-1$ trials with $r-1$ successes: $P(X=k)=P(\text{first }k-1\text{ trials with exactly }r-1\text{ successes})$
$=P(r-1\text{ successes from }k-1\text{ trials})\cdot P(k^{\text{th}}\text{ success})$
$$=\dbinom{k-1}{r-1}\cdot p^{r-1}(1-p)^{(k-1)-(r-1)}=\text{pmf}$$
Proof: Check that it sums to equal 1.

$$\sum^\infty_{k=r}1(X=k)=1$$
