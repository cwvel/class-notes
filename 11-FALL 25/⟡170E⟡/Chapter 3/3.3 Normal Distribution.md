### Definition
RV $X$ has a **normal distribution** if its pdf is defined by
$$f(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\left[-\dfrac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right],\qquad-\infty<x<\infty$$
with $-\infty<\mu<\infty$ and $0<\sigma<\infty$. Then $$X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)$$

### MGF
$$M(t)=\text{exp}\left(\mu t+\dfrac{\sigma^2 t^2}{2}\right)$$
$$M'(t)=(\mu+\sigma^2 t)\exp\left(\mu t + \dfrac{\sigma^2 t^2}{2}\right)$$
$$M''(t)=[(\mu+\sigma^2 t)^2+\sigma^2]\exp\left(\mu t+\dfrac{\sigma^2t^2}{2}\right)$$

### Standard Normal Distribution
If $Z\sim N(\mu=0,\sigma^2=1)$, we say $Z$ has a **standard normal distribution** with a pdf of
$$f_Z(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\left[{-\dfrac{x^2}{2}}\right]dx$$
Then the cdf of $Z$ is 
$$\phi(z)=P(Z\leq z)=\int_{-\infty}^z\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-w^2/2}dw$$
The integral cannot be evaluated and we instead use tabled numerical approximations.
It is symmetrical and for all real $z$,
$$\phi(-z)=P(Z\leq-z)=P(Z>z)=1-\phi(z)$$
#### Standardization
If $X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)$, then $Z=(X-\mu)/\sigma\sim N(0,1)$ and is called the **standardization** or **standard score** of $X$.

If $X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)$, then $V=(X-\mu)^2/\sigma^2=Z^2$ is $\chi^2(1)$ (chi-squared distribution with df=1).

### Percentile
The $(100p)$-th percentile, $\pi_p$, for a random variable $X$ is a number such that $P(X\leq\pi_p)=p$.

If $Z\sim N(1,0)$ then 
$$P(Z\geq z_\alpha)=\alpha,\qquad P(Z<z_\alpha)=1-\alpha$$

*Notes*
>$$\int_\mathbb{R}e^{-x^2}dx=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{-x^2}dx=\sqrt\pi$$

## Standard normal distribution
$$Z\sim N(\mu=0,\sigma^2=1)$$
$$f_Z(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\dfrac{x^2}{2}}dx$$
$Z$ is called the **standard normal distribution (SND)**
$$Z\sim\text{SN}(0,1)$$
$$\Phi(x)=P(Z\leq x)=\int_{-\infty}^x\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\dfrac{x^2}{2}}dx$$

### Definition
$$Z=\dfrac{X=\mu}{\sigma}$$
$$\implies F_Z(x)=P(Z\leq x)=P\left(\dfrac{X-\mu}{\sigma}\leq x\right)$$
$$=P\left(X\leq\sigma x+\mu\right)=\int_{-\infty}^{\sigma x+\mu}\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}e^{-\left(\dfrac{x-\mu}{\sqrt2 \sigma}\right)^2}dx$$
$Z=\dfrac{t-\mu}{\sigma}\implies d=\dfrac{dt}{\sigma}$
$$F_Z(x)=\dots=\int_{-\infty}^x\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}e^{-\dfrac{z^2}{2}}\sigma dz=\int_{-\infty}^x\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\dfrac{z^2}{2}} dz=\phi(z)$$
For the standard normal distribution,
$$f(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\cdot e^{-\dfrac{x^2}{2}}$$
$$\phi(-z)=P(Z\leq -z),\qquad P(Z>z)=1-P(Z\leq z)=1-\phi(z)$$
