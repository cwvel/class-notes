# Exponential Distribution
>[!definition]
>A continuous RV has an **exponential distribution** if the pdf $f(x)$ is given by, for a fixed $\lambda>0$, 
>$$f(x)=\lambda e^{-\lambda x}$$
>if $x\geq 0$ and $f(x)=0$ if not. The range is $S_x=[0,\infty)$.

### Properties
If $X\sim\text{Exp}(\lambda)$:
1. The total area is 1
$$\int_{\mathbb{R}}f(x)dx\equiv\int_0^{\infty}\lambda e^-{-\lambda x}dx=1$$

2. The mean is
$$\mu=E(x)=\int_\mathbb{R}xf(x)dx=\boxed{\dfrac{1}{\lambda}}$$

3. The variance is $\sigma^2(X)=E(X^2)-(E(X))^2$:
$$E(X^2)=\int_0^\infty x^2f(x)dx=\dfrac{2}{\lambda^2}$$
so
$$\sigma^2(X)==\dfrac{2}{\lambda^2}-(\dfrac{1}{\lambda})^2=\boxed{\dfrac{1}{\lambda^2}}$$

### MGF
$$M_X(t)=E(e^{tx})=\int_0^{\infty}e^{tx}\lambda e^{-\lambda x}dx=\boxed{\dfrac{\lambda}{t-\lambda}},\qquad t>\lambda$$

# Memoryless RV
$X$ is a RV representing the duration of a wait.
$P(X>s)$ is the probability such that the duration exceeds $s$.
$P(X>s+t\mid X>s)$ is the probability that the duration exceeds $s+t$ given that it has exceeded $s$.
$P(X>s+t\mid X>s)=P(X>s)$: restarting of the process

**Ex.** $X\sim \text{Exp}(\lambda)$ is memoryless.
$F(t)=P(X\leq t)=\int_0^t \lambda e^{-\lambda x}dx=1-e^{-\lambda t}$
$P(X>t)=1-P(X\leq t)=1-F(t)=1-(1-e^{-\lambda t})=e^{-\lambda t}$
$P(X>t)=e^{-\lambda t}$
$P(X>s+t\mid X>t)=\dfrac{P(\{X>s+t\}\cap\{X>t\})}{P(X>t)}=\dots = e^{-\lambda s}=P(X>s)$

**Ex.** $X\sim\text{Geom}(p)$ is memoryless.

>[!theorem]
>Suppose that $X$ is a continuous RV n $[0,\infty)$ and $X$ satisfies the memoryless property that for all $t,s>0$, 
>$$P(X>t+s\mid X>t)=P(X>s)$$
>Then it is an exponential distribution and there is a $\lambda$ such that $X\sim \text{Exp}(\lambda)$.

# Gamma Distribution
Comes with the necessity to extend to real numbers.

### Gamma function
$$\Gamma(\alpha)=\int_0^\infty t^{\alpha-1}e^{-t}dt$$
$$\Gamma(1)=\int_0^{\infty}t^0e^{-t}dt=-(e^{-\infty}-e^0)-1=1$$
$$\Gamma(\alpha+1)=\int_0^\infty t^{\alpha}e^{-t}dt$$
Integrating by parts: $u=t^\alpha, dv=e^{-t}dt$
$$=t^\alpha (e^-\infty-e^0)+\int_0^\infty\alpha t^{\alpha-1}e^{-t}dt=\alpha\int_0^\infty t^{\alpha-1}e^{-t}dt$$
So
$$\Gamma(\alpha + 1)=\alpha\int_0^\infty t^{\alpha-1}e^{-t}dt\equiv \alpha\cdot\Gamma(\alpha)$$
$$=\alpha(\alpha-1)\Gamma(\alpha-1)$$
$$=\alpha(\alpha-1)(\alpha-2)\Gamma(\alpha-2)$$
$$=\dots=\alpha(\alpha-1)(\alpha-2)\dots\Gamma(1)$$
$$\implies\Gamma(\alpha+1)=\alpha(\alpha-1)(\alpha-2)\dots(1)=\alpha!$$
>[!definition]
>Gamma distribution
$$\Gamma(x)=\int_0^{\infty}t^{x-1}e^{-t}dt$$
$$\Gamma(x+1)=x\Gamma(x)=x!$$

>[!definition]
>The pdf of a Gamma distribution $X\sim\text{Gamm}(k,\lambda)$:
>$$f_x(x)=f(n)=\dfrac{\lambda^k}{\Gamma(k)}x^{k-1}e^{-\lambda x}$$
>for $x>0$ and $0$ if $x\leq 0$.

For $k=1$:
$$\dfrac{\lambda^1}{\Gamma(1)}x^0e^{-\lambda x}=\lambda e^{-\lambda x}\Longrightarrow X\sim\text{exp}(\lambda)$$

Another notation:
$\theta=\dfrac{1}{\lambda}\implies\boxed{\lambda=\dfrac{1}{\theta}}$
$$f(x)=\dfrac{1}{\Gamma(k)}\dfrac{1}{\theta^k}x^{k-1}e^{-\frac{x}{\theta}},\qquad X\sim\text{Gamma}(k,\theta)$$

>[!definition] Properties
>1. pmf should sum to 1 (can take $\Gamma(k)$ out of the integral)
>$$\int_0^\infty\dfrac{\lambda^k}{\Gamma(k)}x^{k-1}e^{-\lambda x}dx=\dfrac{\lambda^k}{\Gamma(k)}\int_0^\infty x^{k-1}e^{-\lambda x}dx=\int_\mathbb{R}f(x)dx=1$$
>$\quad$

### Application
Let's assume that $V$ is a random variable representing the arrival time of the $\alpha^\text{th}$ guest.
cdf of $V$: $$P(V\leq t)=1-P(V>t)=1-P(\text{"At most $\alpha-1$ arrivals before that"})$$
$$=1-P(N[0,t]\leq \alpha-1)=1-\sum_0^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda t}\dfrac{(\lambda t)^k}{k!}$$
$$F(t)=P(V\leq t)=F(t)=1-\sum_{k=0}^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda t}\dfrac{(\lambda t)^k}{k!}$$
pdf of $V$:
$$F'(t)=\dfrac{d}{dt}(1-\sum_{k=0}^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda t}\dfrac{(\lambda t)^k}{k!})$$
$$f(t)=F'(t)=\dfrac{t^{\alpha -1}e^{-t/\frac{1}{\lambda}}}{\Gamma(\alpha)(\dfrac{1}{\lambda})^\alpha}\equiv\text{Gamma}(\alpha,\theta=\dfrac{1}{\lambda})$$
So $V\sim\text{Gamma}(\alpha,\theta=\dfrac{1}{\lambda})$
1st arrival $\sim\text{Exp}(\lambda)$ and 2nd arrival $\sim\text{Gamma}(\alpha,\dfrac{1}{\lambda})$
Mean: $\mu=\alpha\theta=\dfrac{\alpha}{\lambda}$
Variance: $\sigma^2=\dfrac{\alpha}{\lambda^2}$


### Expected value
$$E(X_G)=\int_\mathbb{R}xf(x)dx=\int_0^{\infty}x\cdot\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}\cdot x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda x}dx$$
$$=\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}\int_0^\infty x^\alpha e^{-\lambda x}dx$$
Change of variables $t=\lambda x\rightarrow x=\dfrac{t}{\lambda}\rightarrow dx=\dfrac{dt}{\lambda}$
$$E(X_G)=\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}\int_0^\infty (\dfrac{t}{\lambda})^\alpha\cdot \dfrac{e^{-t}dt}{\lambda}=\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\lambda^{\alpha +1}\Gamma(\alpha)}\int_0^\infty t^\alpha e^{-t}dt$$
$$=\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\lambda^{\alpha+1}\Gamma(\alpha)}\Gamma(\alpha+1)=\dfrac{1}{\lambda \cdot\Gamma(\alpha)}\cdot \alpha\Gamma(\alpha)=\boxed{\dfrac{\alpha}{\lambda}}$$

### Variance
$$E(X^2)=\int_\mathbb{R}x^2f(x)dx=\int_0^\infty x^2\cdot\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}\cdot x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda x}dx$$
$$=\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}\int_0^\infty x^{\alpha +1}e^{-\lambda x}dx$$
Change of variables $t=\lambda x\rightarrow x=\dfrac{t}{\lambda}\rightarrow dx=\dfrac{dt}{\lambda}$
$$E(X^2)=\dots=\dfrac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)\lambda^{\alpha +2}}\cdot \Gamma(\alpha+2)=\dots=\dfrac{\alpha(\alpha+1)}{\lambda^2}$$
Then
$$\sigma^2(X_G)=\dfrac{\alpha(\alpha+1)}{\lambda^2}-(\dfrac{\alpha}{\lambda})^2$$
$$\boxed{\sigma^2=\dfrac{\alpha}{\lambda^2}}$$
### Chi-squared distribution
Setting $\alpha=\dfrac{r}{2}$ and $\lambda=\dfrac{1}{2}$: $X\sim\text{Gamma}(\alpha=\dfrac{r}{2},\lambda=\dfrac{1}{2})$
$$f(x)=\dfrac{x^{\frac{\lambda}{2}-1}e^{-\frac{x}{2}}}{\Gamma(\frac{\lambda}{2})\cdot \alpha^{\frac{\lambda}{2}}}\qquad\text{when $x>0$, and $0$ if $x\leq 0$}$$
This is the probability density function of the **chi-squared distribution**
$X\sim X^2(r)$ is the number of degrees of freedom of this pdf:
$$E(X)=r,\qquad \sigma^2(X)=2r$$
