Consider $(X,Y)$ with joint pmf $f(x,y)$ and marginal $f_X$ and $f_Y$.
Define:
$$A=\{X=x\}\qquad\text{and}\qquad B=\{Y=y\}$$
Then
$$P(A\mid B)=\dfrac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}=\dfrac{P(X=x,Y=y)}{P(Y=y)}=\dfrac{f(x,y)}{f_Y(y)}\qquad\text{if }f_Y(y)>0$$
>[!definition]
>The conditional pmf of $X$ given that $Y=y$ is defined
>$$g(x\mid y)=\dfrac{f(x,y)}{f_Y(y)},\quad f_Y(y)>0$$
>Likewise, the conditional pmf of $Y$ given $X=x$ is given by
>$$h(y\mid x)=\dfrac{f(x,y)}{f_X(x)},\quad f_X(x)>0$$
>These conditional pmfs satisfy $$\sum_{S_X}g(x\mid y)=1\quad\text{and}\quad\sum_{S_Y}h(y\mid x)=1$$

>[!definition]
>The **conditional expectation** of $X$ given $\{Y=y\}$ is $$E(X\mid Y=y)=E(X\mid Y)=\sum_{S_x}x\cdot g(x\mid y)$$
>Then
>$$\mu_{X\mid y}=E(X\mid y)$$
>$$\sigma^2(X\mid y)=E((X-\mu_{X\mid y})^2\mid y)=E(X^2\mid y)-(\mu_{X\mid y})^2$$

### Conditional Expectation as RV
Given $(X,Y)$ jointly distributed, defined
$$\mu(x)=E(Y\mid x)=E(Y\mid X=x)$$
Thus, $\mu(X)\sim E(Y\mid x)$ is a random variable in the space of values $S=\{E(Y\mid x),x\in S_X\}$, with $P(\mu(X)\mid E(Y\mid x))=P(X=x)$.
