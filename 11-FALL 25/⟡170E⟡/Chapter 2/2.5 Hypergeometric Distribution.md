>[!definition]
>The **hypergeometric distribution** is a probability distribution that describes the likelihood of drawing a certain number of "successes" from a finite population *without replacement*.

**Ex.** Draw $n=5$ cards from a deck of 52 without replacement. What's the probability of getting exactly $k=2$ hearts?
$$P(X=2)=\dfrac{\binom{13}{2}\binom{39}{3}}{\binom{52}{5}}$$
>[!definition]
>Suppose you have
>a) Population of size $N=N_1+N_2$
>b) Contains $K$ successes ("good" items), $K=N_1$
>c) You draw $n$ items without replacement
>d) Observation: $X$ successes drawn.
>Then $X\sim\text{Hgeom}(N_1+N_2,K,n)$
>$$\text{pmf}\equiv P(X=k)=\dfrac{\binom{N_1}{k}\binom{N_2}{n-k}}{\binom{N_1+N_2}{n}}$$

### Expected value
For $\text{pmf}=P(X=X=k)$, $k=0,1,2,\dots$, the expected value of $X$:
$$\mu=E(X)=\sum_{k\in S_i}\dfrac{k\binom{N_1}{k}\binom{N_2}{n-k}}{\binom{N}{n}}$$
$$=\sum\dfrac{k\cdot\dfrac{N_1!}{k!(N_1-k)!}\cdot\dbinom{N_2}{n-k}}{(\dfrac{N}{n})\cdot\dbinom{N-1}{n-1}}$$
$$=\sum\dfrac{n\cdot N_1}{N}\cdot\dfrac{\dfrac{(N_1-1)!}{(k-1)!(N_1-k)!}\cdot\dbinom{N_2}{n-k}}{\dbinom{N-1}{n-1}}$$
$$\implies\boxed{\mu=E(X)=n\cdot\dfrac{N_1}{N}\cdot\sum_{k\in S_x, k\neq 0}\dfrac{\dbinom{N_1-1}{k-1}\dbinom{N_2}{n-k}}{\dbinom{N-1}{n-1}}}$$
If we take $P(Y=k-1),y\sim\text{Hgeom}(N-1,k,n)$
$$\sum_{k\in S}\dfrac{\dbinom{N_1-1}{k-1}\dbinom{N_2}{(n-1)-(k-1)}}{\dbinom{N-1}{n-1}}=P(Y=k-1)=1$$
Since the sum across the pmf must be 1. Then
$$\implies \boxed{ \mu=E(X)=n\cdot\dfrac{N_1}{N}}$$
### Variance
$V(X)=E((X-\mu)^2)=E(X^2)-(E(X))^2=M''_X(t=0)-(M'_X(t=0))^2$
$$\boxed{V(X)=n(\dfrac{N_1}{N})(\dfrac{N_2}{N})(\dfrac{N-n}{N-1})}$$
Proof in textbook.

### With replacement
$E(X)=n\cdot(\dfrac{N_1}{N})$ and $V(X)=n\cdot(\dfrac{N_1}{N})(\dfrac{N_2}{N})$
